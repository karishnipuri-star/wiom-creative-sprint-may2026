▣ Ground Test · Field Research

Can a single creative land
home internet and recharge flexibility together?

Four of us went on the ground to test whether a single creative could land two things: that all devices run on the same net (home internet), and that recharge is as per the user (flexibility).

🖼 3 Static creatives ▶ 1 Video creative
Objective

Testing a message to land two points

And check the response.

🏠
Point 1 — Home internet
All devices will be running on the same net.
⚡
Point 2 — Flexibility
Recharge is as per the user.
What we found

Key Learnings

The creative succeeded at explaining the recharge mechanic but failed to anchor the product category. Flexibility landed. Home internet did not.

Confirmed

Recharge flexibility proposition landed

31/44 could describe the recharge flexibility without being prompted — the mechanic landed even when the category didn't. Of the 17 who correctly identified this as home internet, 10 also picked up the flexibility message. When the category lands, the value prop travels with it. "1 din / 7 din" was paraphrased back verbatim.

Gap

"Ghar ka net" is not striking enough

Only 18% (8/44) identified the ad as WiFi. Of the 17 who said "net," most leaned straight into recharge as what was different — the home internet framing isn't registering as the anchor.

Gap

Category identification consumed the bandwidth — use-case examples added to the load

When people don't yet know what the product is, they're spending all their cognitive effort resolving that. The match and exam examples arrived on top of that unresolved question and added more load instead of answering it. 4 respondents became visibly confused — "match" was treated as the product context, not a usage illustration.

Insight

Existing recharge and WiFi schemas hijacked meaning

"WiFi" and "recharge" are not empty labels — they arrive pre-loaded. "WiFi" carries {all devices, home, unlimited, monthly, ISP comparison}; "recharge" carries {mobile, small pack, single device, prepaid}. Users don't build a new category from these words — they reconcile two schemas they already own. Without a strong category anchor in the creative, the ad got pulled into both simultaneously, and landed in neither cleanly.

Insight

Copy decoded, category not inferred

~23% paraphrased the lines verbatim but could not identify the category. Mani reproduced "jitne din chahiye utne din wala" word for word — then said "samajh nahi aaya." Linguistic comprehension does not equal category understanding.

What's next

Open questions

We have three questions to answer before the next sprint. The path of experimentation depends on getting these right.

Q1

When we say "ghar ka net," what exactly do we want to land?

The phrase is doing too much at once. We need to pick one.

Monthly subscription model? All devices on the same net? Unlimited usage?
Q2

Who is our audience — and what do we mean by "WiFi considerer"?

The segment label is not sharp enough to brief a creative. We need to define it.

When we say "WiFi considerer," do we mean someone who understands WiFi? And if so — which part of WiFi? The device? The connectivity? The pricing model? The category of home internet itself?

Q3

What is the performance ad trying to do — and in what order do we solve?

Two questions that need one answer together.

Is the ad's job to bring users to the app, or to build category understanding first? And once we know that — do we solve category, then differentiation, then examples in sequence, or does the performance ad carry all three? Right now it tries to do all three at once and none land fully. The order matters.

The call we have to take

The objective is three coupled choices. We cannot write a brief until these are answered.

1

Which wrong model do we suppress — mobile recharge, or broadband-monthly? This is audience-dependent. It requires a diagnostic on which schema fragment the audience is already holding before we can answer it.

2

Which single belief converts — and what is the gate? Likely: whole-home connectivity is the gate (contamination control) and recharge-flexibility is the value prop we sell past it. For a WiFi-considering audience that split may flip. This needs to be decided, not assumed.

3

At which stage of the performance funnel does each job land? What is the ad's job versus the install, booking flow, and fee step's job — and does the current creative carry the right load for where it sits?
